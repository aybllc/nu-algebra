Definition of Closure (8th–grade friendly)

Closure means:
When you do an operation (like add or multiply) inside a system, you always land back inside the system.

Example: If you start with whole numbers and add them (2+3=5), you still get a whole number. That means whole numbers are “closed under addition.”
