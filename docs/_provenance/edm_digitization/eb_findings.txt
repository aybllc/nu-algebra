FINDING 1 — Inverse relationship as conserved quantity, with slack
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
Each session contains 5 trials. For Set 1 sessions:
    e_IDKPTM(Set1) + e_correct + slack = 5,   slack ≥ 0
Slack = trials in the third response class (incorrect, no response,
        or off-task) — not separately tracked in the original coding.

Per-child slack profile across intervention (Set 1 sessions only):

  Child    N    mean slack   zero-slack     max   
  Alex     41   1.27         16/41 (39%)   5     
  Aden     28   0.11         25/28 (89%)   1     
  Ida      14   1.07         6/14 (43%)   4     

Reading: Aden showed near-zero slack throughout intervention (89% of
sessions had zero third-class trials), indicating a clean transition from
the trained mand to the correct intraverbal. Alex (39%) and Ida (43%) had
substantially more third-class responding — slack rates that the standard
coding did not separately surface.


FINDING 2 — Stimulus-control onset locatable to a session
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
Operationalisation: first session s* such that for all s ≥ s*,
    e_IDKPTM(s) + e_correct(s) ≥ 4   (lower-bound on the conserved sum)
This is the first session at which the child is reliably producing either
the trained mand or the correct intraverbal on at least 4 of 5 trials —
and continues to do so for the remainder of intervention.

  Alex: never reached strict criterion (high variability)
           soft criterion (≥85% of tail sessions sum ≥ 4): session 22
  Aden: stimulus-control onset at session 10
  Ida: stimulus-control onset at session 15

Reading: Aden achieved stimulus control immediately upon intervention onset
(session 10), Ida at session 15. Alex's high session-to-session variability
means he never satisfies the strict criterion — a structural finding the
standard mastery-criterion lens does not surface.


FINDING 3 — Aden's six-week break as a maintenance probe
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
The break occurred between sessions 32 and 33 (figure asterisk position).
Aden's IDKPTM had ALREADY been declining before the break as he learned
answers — so the break is not a clean asymptote-vs-asymptote comparison.
It is, however, a 6-week interruption of an active learning curve.

  Pre-break  (sessions 30-32) IDKPTM: [2, 2, 4] (= [40, 40, 80]%)
  Post-break (sessions 33-35) IDKPTM: [2, 2, 2] (= [40, 40, 40]%)

EB encoding (Reading R1, range-faithful):
  Pre  = (60, 20)    hull = [40, 80]
  Post = (40, 0)    hull = [40, 40]
  ΔAden = Post ⊕ ((-1) ⊙ Pre) = (-20, 20)
  Hull of ΔAden = [-40, 0]

Reading: The six-week interruption maps to ΔAden = (-20, 20) with hull
[-40, 0]. The centre is a 20-percentage-point drop in IDKPTM rate; the
hull excludes any gain (right edge sits at zero) and admits up to a 40-point
decline. The signed-residual reading places this firmly on the EB carrier's
signed half — the operator-level B²≠id diagnostic activates here.
Important caveat: pre-break IDKPTM was already declining as Aden learned
answers, so this does NOT cleanly isolate the break-induced decrement from
the ongoing learning trajectory. The figure-derived comparison would benefit
from per-trial timing data to separate within-session variability from
across-break decay.
