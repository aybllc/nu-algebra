The standard mandates explicit uncertainty, non‑negativity, and invariant‑preserving transformations, reusing the algebra’s proven operators for addition, multiplication, scalar scaling, and two governance primitives (“Catch” and “Flip”). These operators guarantee closure, monotonicity, sub‑distributivity, and associative composition of the nominal part while keeping uncertainty conservative and auditable with O(1) per operation and O(m) for chains, validated over >70{,}000 test cases. We formalize a convergence theorem (proposed) showing that, under minimal axioms for ethical composition and audit cost, repeated governance updates converge to the N/U form as a fixed point that minimizes epistemic entropy subject to structural constraints. The result yields an implementable, standardizable guardrail for AI integration in regulated domains. ￼ ￼ ￼ ￼ ⸻ Table of Contents (headings only) 1. Motivation and Scope 2. Abstract (150 words)

We propose a governance standard that operationalizes Nominal/Uncertainty (N/U) algebra—representing every reportable quantity as a pair (n,u) with u \ge 0—as a universal control layer for AI systems and public‑facing decisions. The standard mandates explicit uncertainty, non‑negativity, and invariant‑preserving transformations, reusing the algebra’s proven operators for addition, multiplication, scalar scaling, and two governance primitives (“Catch” and “Flip”). These operators guarantee closure, monotonicity, sub‑distributivity, and associative composition of the nominal part while keeping uncertainty conservative and auditable with O(1) per operation and O(m) for chains, validated over >70{,}000 test cases. We formalize a convergence theorem (proposed) showing that, under minimal axioms for ethical composition and audit cost, repeated governance updates converge to the N/U form as a fixed point that minimizes epistemic entropy subject to structural constraints. The result yields an implementable, standardizable guardrail for AI integration in regulated domains.  ￼  ￼  ￼  ￼

4) Convergence Between Epistemic Entropy and Structural Necessity

Status: Proposed theorem (theory; needs peer review).

4.1 Definitions
    •    Decision space: A=\mathbb{R}\times\mathbb{R}_{\ge0}.
    •    Epistemic entropy: H_E: A\to\mathbb{R}_{\ge0}, monotone in u and Schur‑convex w.r.t. uncertainty aggregation (e.g., H_E(n,u)=h(u) with h’ \ge 0). (Speculative choice; any monotone, subadditive information measure is admissible.)
    •    Structural necessity: the set \mathcal{S} of binary operations \circ on A satisfying:
    1. (2–6 sourced by N/U properties/validation; 6 by complexity claims.)  ￼  ￼  ￼

4.2 Update Operator (governance cycle)
    •    Define G_\theta acting on a pipeline \mathcal{P} of composed decisions by: (i) replace each composition with a member of \mathcal{S}, (ii) apply Catch when u>\theta, (iii) optional Flip for adversarial review, (iv) recompute invariants/logs. G_\theta maps pipelines to pipelines.

4.3 Theorem (Convergence, fixed‑point characterization) — Proposed
    •    Claim: For any pipeline with bounded evidence assimilation (i.e., updates never increase u without recorded evidence) and any entropy H_E monotone in u, the iterates G_\theta^{(t)}(\mathcal{P}_0) produce a non‑increasing sequence of global entropies H_E^\ast(t) that converges. Moreover, any limit point whose operations satisfy 1–6 above is equivalent to N/U composition (⊕, ⊗, ⊙; Catch; Flip) up to isomorphism on A, and minimizes H_E among conservative first‑order bounds subject to those constraints.
    •    Sketch:
    •    Monotone convergence: Catch enforces u\le\theta locally; monotonicity of H_E in u yields H_E^\ast(t+1)\le H_E^\ast(t); bounded below by 0 ⇒ convergence.
    •    Minimality: Among conservative first‑order bounds satisfying 1–6, uncertainty must propagate additively in u with nominal‑sensitivity weights. Symmetry, non‑negativity, and local linearity force the unique bilinear form |n_1|u_2+|n_2|u_1 for products; sums are (u_1+u_2); scalars |a|u. (This mirrors the validated definitions in the NASA paper; uniqueness claim is theoretical and to be proved formally.)  ￼
    •    Fixed point: When all compositions are N/U‑consistent and all u\le\theta or routed via Catch, G_\theta becomes idempotent → fixed point.

4.4 Corollary (Governance optimality) — Proposed
    •    Under the axioms, N/U governance attains the lowest epistemic entropy compatible with conservative auditability and O(1) cost, explaining its structural necessity in safety‑critical settings. (Speculative; to be empirically benchmarked.)

\section{Auditable AI Reasoning Framework}

\begin{definition}[Reasoning State Space]
Let $\mathcal{R} = \mathbb{R} \times \mathbb{R}_{\geq 0} \times \mathcal{P}$ be the \emph{auditable reasoning space}, where elements are triples $(n, u, \rho)$ with:
\begin{itemize}
    \item $n \in \mathbb{R}$: nominal estimate (best judgment)
    \item $u \geq 0$: epistemic uncertainty bound
    \item $\rho \in \mathcal{P}$: provenance (audit trail)
\end{itemize}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Uncertainty Invariant]
The \emph{information measure} $M: \mathcal{R} \to \mathbb{R}_{\geq 0}$ is defined by:
\begin{equation}
M(n, u, \rho) = |n| + u
\end{equation}
This represents total ``probability mass plus epistemic uncertainty.''
\end{definition}

\section{Reasoning Operations}

\begin{definition}[Conjunctive Composition]
For independent reasoning steps $r_1 = (n_1, u_1, \rho_1)$ and $r_2 = (n_2, u_2, \rho_2)$, the \emph{conjunctive combination} (both must hold) is:
\begin{equation}
r_1 \otimes_{\land} r_2 = (n_1 \cdot n_2, \, |n_1|u_2 + |n_2|u_1, \, \rho_1 \cup \rho_2)
\end{equation}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Disjunctive Composition]
For independent reasoning paths $r_1, \ldots, r_k$, the \emph{disjunctive combination} (at least one succeeds) is:
\begin{equation}
\bigoplus_{i=1}^{k} r_i = \left(1 - \prod_{i=1}^{k}(1 - n_i), \, f_u(\{u_i\}, \{n_i\}), \, \bigcup_{i=1}^{k} \rho_i\right)
\end{equation}
where $f_u$ is a conservative uncertainty aggregation function satisfying:
\begin{equation}
f_u(\{u_i\}, \{n_i\}) \geq \max\{u_1, \ldots, u_k\}
\end{equation}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Linear Combination]
For reasoning steps with additive structure:
\begin{equation}
r_1 \oplus r_2 = (n_1 + n_2, \, u_1 + u_2, \, \rho_1 \cup \rho_2)
\end{equation}
\end{definition}

\section{Governance Operators}

\begin{definition}[Catch Operator]
For threshold $\alpha > 0$, the \emph{Catch operator} $C_\alpha: \mathcal{R} \to \mathcal{R} \cup \{\text{AUDIT}\}$ is:
\begin{equation}
C_\alpha(n, u, \rho) = \begin{cases}
(n, u, \rho) & \text{if } u \leq \alpha \\
(0, |n| + u, \rho \cup \{\text{high-uncertainty}\}) & \text{if } u > \alpha
\end{cases}
\end{equation}
When $u > \alpha$, the system triggers \texttt{AUDIT} flag for human review.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Invariant Preservation]
The Catch operator preserves the information measure:
\begin{equation}
M(C_\alpha(n, u, \rho)) = M(n, u, \rho) \quad \forall \alpha
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Case 1: $u \leq \alpha$. Then $C_\alpha(n,u,\rho) = (n,u,\rho)$ and $M$ unchanged.

Case 2: $u > \alpha$. Then $C_\alpha(n,u,\rho) = (0, |n|+u, \rho')$.
\begin{align}
M(C_\alpha(n,u,\rho)) &= |0| + (|n| + u) \\
&= |n| + u \\
&= M(n,u,\rho)
\end{align}
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Flip Operator]
The \emph{Flip operator} $B: \mathcal{R} \to \mathcal{R}$ swaps nominal and uncertainty for adversarial review:
\begin{equation}
B(n, u, \rho) = (u, |n|, \rho \cup \{\text{meta-uncertainty}\})
\end{equation}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Flip Preserves Invariant]
\begin{equation}
M(B(n, u, \rho)) = M(n, u, \rho)
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\section{Audit Protocol}

\begin{algorithm}[Reasoning Verification]
Given a reasoning chain $\{r_0, r_1, \ldots, r_T\}$ with operations $\{op_1, \ldots, op_T\}$, verify:

\textbf{Input:} Reasoning trace $(r_0, op_1, r_1, op_2, \ldots, op_T, r_T)$

\textbf{Checks:}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Operation Correctness:} For each $t$, verify $r_t = op_t(r_{t-1})$ or $op_t(r_{t-1}, r_{t-1}^{\text{aux}})$
    \item \textbf{Invariant Conservation:} $M(r_t) \geq M(r_{t-1})$ with equality for Catch/Flip
    \item \textbf{Non-negativity:} $u_t \geq 0$ for all $t$
    \item \textbf{Monotonicity:} If $u_{t-1}' \geq u_{t-1}$, then $u_t' \geq u_t$
    \item \textbf{Catch Triggers:} If $u_t > \alpha$, verify $C_\alpha$ was invoked
    \item \textbf{Provenance Chain:} $\rho_t \supseteq \rho_{t-1}$ (audit trail grows)
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Output:} $\{\texttt{VERIFIED}, \texttt{FAILED}\}$ with error location if failed
\end{algorithm}

\begin{theorem}[Auditability]

\begin{equation}

\end{theorem}
