Nominal/Uncertainty Algebra as a Companion Method for Psychological Science: A Formal Case and Implementation Blueprint

Abstract.
Psychological science has invested decades in refining inferential models while leaving a gap at the level where most analyses actually live: arithmetic over uncertain quantities. Nominal/Uncertainty (N/U) algebra fills this gap by carrying a conservative uncertainty radius alongside every nominal value and propagating that radius through the exact arithmetic researchers already perform—contrasts, linear rescaling, products, and study‑level pooling. In this paper, we argue that N/U algebra should be standardized as a companion method that runs in parallel to frequentist and Bayesian pipelines. We first document how uncertainty is currently propagated in psychology (Gaussian root‑sum‑squares, confidence intervals, inverse‑variance meta‑analysis; Bayes factors and hierarchical meta‑analysis; robustness practices including p‑curve, multiverse, many‑analysts, and reproducibility projects). We then formalize the N/U framework from The NASA Paper and Small Falcon Algebra (Martin, 2025)—our single source of truth for definitions, proofs, and invariants—and establish theoretical and computational properties: closure, commutativity, associativity, monotonicity, invariant conservation, and O(1) per‑operation complexity. Using a verified numerical archive, we show that N/U produces strictly conservative yet non‑inflated bounds across >70,000 cases and remains stable under chaining; its uncertainty radii exceed first‑order Gaussian propagation by minimal margins and coincide with interval arithmetic for nonnegative nominals within floating‑point error. The paper concludes with implementation guidance for contrasts, composites, transforms, and meta‑analysis, and with demonstrations on open psychology datasets. We recommend that journals and preregistrations adopt N/U algebra as a reporting standard next to confidence intervals and Bayes factors. N/U algebra is prepared to serve as a conservative companion method in psychology, ensuring transparency and robustness.

1. Introduction: why psychology needs an algebra of uncertainty at the level of computation

Psychological research is increasingly explicit about modeling assumptions, preregistration, and robustness diagnostics. Yet the discipline still lacks a standardized, assumption‑light method for tracking how uncertainty moves through the arithmetic steps that underlie nearly every analysis: adding and subtracting means, multiplying by scalars to rescale, forming indices and weighted composites, and aggregating across studies. Probabilistic frameworks answer “what follows if the model is true?”; robustness projects answer “how brittle are results to analytic choices?”; but neither, by themselves, provides a deterministic guardrail for the arithmetic itself. N/U algebra addresses this missing layer by propagating a nonnegative uncertainty bound in lockstep with the nominal value of every quantity, yielding conservative envelopes that are trivial to audit and O(1) to compute at each step. The goal is not to replace frequentist or Bayesian practice but to complement it with a companion calculation that certifies whether a qualitative claim (direction, equivalence, practical magnitude) survives worst‑case bounded error through the exact computation chain.

2. Current practices in uncertainty propagation (frequentist, Bayesian, robustness)

Psychological science typically represents study‑level uncertainty by standard errors and confidence intervals, propagating error under Gaussian or delta‑method approximations, and synthesizes evidence by inverse‑variance weighting in fixed or random effects meta‑analysis. Heterogeneity statistics (Q, I²), small‑k corrections (e.g., HKSJ), funnel‑plot asymmetry tests, and selection adjustments extend these models. Bayesian practice contributes Bayes factors for common designs, hierarchical pooling with priors on heterogeneity, and model averaging (including selection models) in meta‑analysis. Robustness/meta‑science has emphasized p‑curve, multiverse analyses, many‑analysts projects, registered reports, and calls to de‑emphasize dichotomous “significance.” These advances diffuse model‑based uncertainty and analytic flexibility; they do not, however, supply a formal calculus for worst‑case propagation through the arithmetic steps of one’s pipeline. The companion method proposed here fills precisely that gap, leaving inferential choices intact while guaranteeing transparent, assumption‑light envelopes around the computations themselves.

8. Limitations and scope

N/U algebra deliberately declines to model covariance structures or distributional tails; it provides deterministic envelopes for computation, not a full inferential calculus. Where dependence carries crucial scientific meaning, conventional models remain essential. The role of N/U is to certify that the arithmetic of your analysis does not smuggle in optimism. It is intentionally conservative: when N/U flags fragility (

9. Conclusion

Psychology has matured robust workflows for modeling and for checking robustness to analytic choices. What has been missing is a method that makes the arithmetic itself transparent, conservative, and auditable. N/U algebra offers exactly that: a minimal, closed‑form calculus that rides in parallel with existing pipelines and tells you whether the sign, magnitude, or equivalence of your claims could change under worst‑case bounded error. We therefore recommend that journals and preregistrations adopt an N/U companion panel across contrasts, composites, transforms, and meta‑analysis. N/U algebra is prepared to serve as a conservative companion method in psychology, ensuring transparency and robustness.
