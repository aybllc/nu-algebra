## Purpose

This document is the consolidated scientific roadmap and open-science dissemination plan for the EB Carrier algebra (RSOS-260797 r4) and the Universal Horizon Address (UHA, US 63/902,536) framework, covering all proof / disprove paths and all access pathways for the scientific community.

Two commitments shape this roadmap:

**Commitment 1: We prove or disprove, cleanly.** Every claim has an explicit success criterion, an explicit falsification criterion, and a deposited pre-registration before the test runs. No post-hoc rescue. No moving goalposts.

**Commitment 2: We don't salami-slice. We build foundations.** Each paper is load-bearing for the next. No minimum-publishable-units. No re-publishing the same finding in multiple venues. No splitting a result into method + result + discussion as separate papers. The portfolio is designed so removing any paper would break the structure.

## 2. Anti-salami discipline

### 2.1 Rules

- **Each paper must close a specific, falsifiable claim.** No "method papers" without a result; no "result papers" without a method; no "discussion papers" without new findings.
- **Each paper must reference but not re-derive its predecessors.** The carrier theorem is proven once (in the memoir); subsequent papers cite, do not reproduce.
- **No paper is split unless splitting changes what is being proven.** If two findings rely on the same dataset and the same methodology, they go in one paper unless they answer two different questions.
- **No re-submission of the same finding in different venues.** A negative result is published once. A positive result is published once. Translation pieces (e.g., trade-book chapter, online explainer) are not republications and do not count toward the portfolio.

### 2.2 Why this matters here

The temptation to salami-slice is high because the framework spans pure math, applied cosmology, behavioral science, and engineering. Each domain has its own venue conventions and the same underlying result could plausibly be retold three or four ways. Resisting this is what makes the work a foundation rather than a publication count.

### 2.3 Test for any candidate paper

Before drafting a paper, three questions must pass:

1. What specific claim does this paper close that no existing paper closes?
2. If this paper is removed from the portfolio, what subsequent work becomes impossible or unmotivated?
3. Could this paper's content be added as a section to an existing paper without forcing the existing paper to bloat?

If question 2 returns "nothing" or question 3 returns "yes, easily," the paper is salami. Don't write it.

## 6. Validation gates and kill switches

The roadmap has explicit kill switches. If any fires, that branch ends and is documented as a clean negative result. The framework is not retreated; the specific empirical claim is dropped.

| Gate | Triggers | If gate fails |
|---|---|---|
| **G_L1** | RSOS-260797 r4 rejected by RSOS | Memoir continues independently; alternative open-access venue (e.g., *Open Mathematics*); algebraic foundation does not depend on RSOS acceptance |
| **G_L3a** | Δχ² = 279 fails to reproduce in Phase 1 of L5 | Companion paper MN-26-1117-P withdrawn or revised; downstream L5 work pauses until reproduction is achieved |
| **G_L3b** | Shared-factor overcounting paper rejected at MN-26-1108-L | Revise and resubmit (already in this state); after second revise-and-resubmit failure, alternative venue search; does not block L5 |
| **G_L5_Phase0** | Riess and Planck used same Ω_m | L5 aborts cleanly; documented as standalone Zenodo negative result; no journal submission attempted |
| **G_L5_Phase2** | UHA Ω_m substitution produces no shift in H_0 | L5 falsified at Phase 2; standalone negative-result paper to MNRAS; further phases not run |
| **G_L5_Phase2_wrong_direction** | Substitution shifts H_0 in opposite direction | L5 falsified cleanly; framework's residual interpretation revised; pre-registration protects against rescue |
| **G_DR1** | Euclid DR1 contradicts locked predictions | predictions falsified cleanly; Phase-4 deposit becomes the canonical falsification record; no pivot to "predictions held in some weaker sense" |
| **G_patent** | Patent attorney advises against pre-registration disclosure | L5 deposit timing adjusted; non-disclosure of UHA Ω_m derivation procedure may force re-scoping of the experiment |

Each gate has a written-out plan for the failure branch. None of the failure plans is "find a different way to publish the same claim." If the gate fails, the claim it gates dies.
